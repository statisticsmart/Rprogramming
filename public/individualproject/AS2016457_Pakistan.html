Corona virus of Pakistan

Introduction

The outbreak of COVID-19 virus initiated in December 2019 in Wuhan, China. At the moment COVID-19 virus is rapidly spreading all the countries of the world. Pakistan also falling prey to COVID-19 epidemic. The first two confirmed COVID-19 cases have reported from Karachi on February 26, 2020 with estimated population of Pakistan as 204.65 million. Currently Pakistan has the third largest number of confirmed cases in Asia after India and Iran, and the 12th highest number of confirmed cases in the world. The number of deaths in the country and case fertility rate have remained relatively low compared to most other countries in Asia and the world. On 20 March, the first death was reported in Sindh. The patient was a 77 years old who had acquired the virus through local transmission.
Pakistan is located in southern Asia. Pakistan is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the south, Iran and Afghanistan to the west, Indian to the east and china to the north.

Visualization of the location of Pakistan

Pakistan has four seasons: a cool, dry winter from December through February; a hot, dry spring from March through May; the summer rainy season or south west monsoon period, from June through September; and the retreating monsoon period of October and November. The mean temperature during June is 38 0C (100 0F) in the plains, the highest temperatures can exceed 47 0C (117 0F). Pakistan recorded one of the highest temperature in the word, 53.7 0C (128.66 0F) on 28 May 2017. The dry, hot weather is broken occasionally by dust storms and thunderstorms that temporarily lower the temperature. Evenings are cool; the daily variation in temperature many be as much as 11 0C to 17 0C. Winters are cold with minimum mean temperatures in Punjab of about 40C (39 0F) in January. Since the COVID-19 pandemic first erupted in china this winter, hopes have lingered that outbreak would die down with the change of seasons. Experts are starting to see a small impact from rising temperature and humidity. It wont stop pandemic, they say but it may help slow down the number of new infections. And the effects of weather may help to explain why parts of the tropics have not been hit quite as hard as temperature regions of the globe. The country was put under a nation-wide lockdown until 9 May, which was initiated on 1st April and later extended twice. Upon its end, the lockdown was eased in phases.
